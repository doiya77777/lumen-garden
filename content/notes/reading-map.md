This page is a lightweight guide for skimming new papers and extracting the main ideas fast.

## Quick pass (5 minutes)
- What problem is solved, and why does it matter now?
- What is actually new: data, model, objective, or evaluation?
- What should change if the claims are true?

## Second pass (20 minutes)
- Identify the main contribution in one sentence.
- Sketch the pipeline on paper: inputs, components, outputs.
- Note the key ablations or comparisons that justify the claim.

## Deep pass
- Reproduce the assumptions in your own words.
- Check for evaluation blind spots (see [[notes/eval-checklist]]).
- List 2 follow-up experiments you would run.
